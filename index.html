Push, Pull, Legs (PPL) Program

Overview

This is a workout program that's recommended for beginners or for people who are just starting their fitness journey. You can do this at home with a pair of adjustable dumbbells and is suitable if you're in an area with limited access to gym equipment.

This program is a decent option for people who don't have time to go the gym and prefers to workout at home. At its core, this workout is essentially a dumbbell-centric routine. Therefore, the exercises are designed to work the advantages of dumbbells. Though there are some exercises (e.g. Squats), where you can opt to use a barbell instead.

Note: While you can expect strength gains from this routine, it's designed more with hypertrophy in mind. To learn more about the difference between hypertrophy and strength training, check out Hypertrophy vs. Strength: What You Should Know.

What is PPL?

PPL is a combination of exercises that promote muscle growth. It's composed of 3 collections of exercises:

  • Push: In the "push" phase, the goal is to train all the upper body pushing muscles (i.e. the chest, shoulders, and triceps).
  • Pull: For "pull", you train all the upper body pulling muscles (e.g. the back and biceps).
  • Legs: Lastly, for the "legs" workout, you train the entire lower body.

Required equipment

  • Dumbbells: You need a range of dumbbells or a pair of adjustable dumbbells. The heavier your dumbbells go, the longer you will be able to make use of this routine.
    • Note: This routine is not a practical option if you only have a single pair of dumbbells.
  • Bench: Ideally you will have access to an adjustable bench. If you only have a flat bench, replace incline fly with flat fly on push day.
  • Pull-up bar: Every gym should have at least some place to do pull-ups. If you are working out at home buy a simple door-mounted bar.
